## 1. FABRICATION INDUSTRY OVERVIEW

### What is Fabrication?
Fabrication is the process of constructing products by combining standardized parts using various manufacturing processes. In heavy industry, this typically involves cutting, bending, welding, and assembling metal structures.

### Key Sectors:
1. **Oil & Gas** - Pressure vessels, pipelines, platforms
2. **Power Generation** - Boilers, heat exchangers, turbine components
3. **Petrochemical** - Reactors, columns, storage tanks
4. **Infrastructure** - Bridges, structural steel
5. **Renewable Energy** - Wind towers, solar structures
6. **Industrial** - Process skids, E-houses, modules

## 2. WELDING METHODS

### 2.1 SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) / Stick Welding
- **Process:** Manual process using consumable electrode coated with flux
- **Applications:** Field welding, repair work, structural steel
- **Pros:** Portable, no shielding gas needed, works in all positions
- **Cons:** Slower, requires slag removal, lower deposition rate
- **Productivity:** 1-3 kg/hour deposition rate

### 2.2 GTAW (Gas Tungsten Arc Welding) / TIG Welding
- **Process:** Non-consumable tungsten electrode with inert gas shield (Argon)
- **Applications:** Root passes, thin materials, stainless steel, aluminum
- **Pros:** High quality, precise control, no spatter
- **Cons:** Slow, requires high skill, expensive equipment
- **Productivity:** 0.5-1.5 kg/hour

### 2.3 GMAW (Gas Metal Arc Welding) / MIG/MAG Welding
- **Process:** Continuous wire feed with shielding gas (CO2/Argon mix)
- **Applications:** Production welding, automotive, general fabrication
- **Pros:** High speed, easy to learn, good for thin materials
- **Cons:** Wind sensitive, equipment cost
- **Productivity:** 2-5 kg/hour

### 2.4 FCAW (Flux-Cored Arc Welding)
- **Process:** Continuous tubular electrode with flux core
- **Types:** Self-shielded (no gas) and gas-shielded
- **Applications:** Heavy fabrication, shipbuilding, field work
- **Pros:** High deposition, portable, good penetration
- **Cons:** More smoke/fumes, slag removal needed
- **Productivity:** 3-8 kg/hour

### 2.5 SAW (Submerged Arc Welding)
- **Process:** Arc submerged under granular flux
- **Applications:** Long straight welds, thick materials, pressure vessels
- **Pros:** Highest deposition rate, excellent quality, no spatter
- **Cons:** Limited to flat/horizontal positions, flux handling
- **Productivity:** 5-15 kg/hour

### 2.6 PAW (Plasma Arc Welding)
- **Process:** Constricted arc through copper nozzle
- **Applications:** Precision work, aerospace, thin materials
- **Pros:** Very concentrated heat, narrow weld zone
- **Cons:** Expensive, complex setup

## 4. PRESSURE VESSEL MANUFACTURING PROCESS

### 4.1 Design & Engineering Phase
1. **ASME Code Review** - Determine applicable code (Section VIII Div 1/2)
2. **Material Selection** - Carbon steel, stainless steel, alloys
3. **Design Calculations** - Thickness, stress analysis
4. ** drawings** - Fabrication drawings, weld details

### 4.2 Material Preparation
1. **Receipt & Inspection** - Mill test certificates, dimensional checks
2. **Storage** - Proper segregation by material grade
3. **Plate Cutting** - Plasma/oxy-fuel cutting to shape
4. **Edge Preparation** - Bevelling for welds

### 4.3 Shell Fabrication
1. **Plate Rolling** - Form cylindrical sections
2. **Longitudinal Seam Welding** - Join rolled plates (usually SAW)
3. **Shell Assembly** - Fit multiple courses
4. **Circumferential Welding** - Join shell sections

### 4.4 Dish Head Fabrication
1. **Blank Cutting** - Circular blanks from plates
2. **Pressing/Forming** - Hydraulic press or spinning
3. **Trimming** - Cut to exact diameter
4. **Nozzle Preparation** - Cut openings

### 4.5 Nozzle & Attachment Welding
1. **Set-on Nozzles** - Weld neck flanges, manways
2. **Reinforcement Pads** - Add repads where required
3. **Support Saddles/Lugs** - Attach mounting hardware
4. **Internals** - Trays, distributors, baffles

### 4.6 Heat Treatment (if required)
1. **Stress Relieving** - PWHT (Post Weld Heat Treatment)
2. **Normalizing** - For carbon steels
3. **Solution Annealing** - For stainless steels

### 4.7 Testing & Inspection
1. **Visual Inspection** - Weld quality check
2. **NDT Methods:**
   - RT (Radiographic Testing)
   - UT (Ultrasonic Testing)
   - MT (Magnetic Particle Testing)
   - PT (Dye Penetrant Testing)
3. **Hydrostatic Test** - 1.3x design pressure
4. **Pneumatic Test** - If hydro not possible
5. **Leak Test** - Soap bubble or sensitive leak detection

### 4.8 Surface Treatment & Coating
1. **Shot Blasting** - SA 2.5 surface finish
2. **Primer Coating** - Shop primer
3. **Insulation** - If required
4. **Final Painting** - Client specified paint system

### 4.9 Documentation & Dispatch
1. **Data Books** - MTRs, NDT reports, test certificates
2. **Nameplate** - ASME stamping
3. **Packing** - Sea-worthy packing for export
4. **Dispatch** - Transport to site

## 5. PROCESS SKID FABRICATION

### 5.1 What is a Process Skid?
Modular, pre-fabricated unit containing process equipment (pumps, vessels, heat exchangers, instrumentation) mounted on a structural steel frame.

### 5.2 Skid Components
1. **Structural Frame** - I-beams, channels, platforms
2. **Equipment** - Vessels, pumps, compressors
3. **Piping** - Process lines, utilities
4. **Instrumentation** - Controls, sensors, valves
5. **Electrical** - MCC, junction boxes, lighting
6. **Utility Systems** - Air, water, nitrogen connections

### 5.3 Fabrication Sequence
1. **Frame Fabrication**
   - Cut structural steel
   - Weld main beams
   - Add cross-members
   - Install floor plates/grating
   - Weld lifting lugs

2. **Equipment Installation**
   - Position vessels/tanks
   - Mount pumps/compressors
   - Install heat exchangers
   - Align and level

3. **Piping Installation**
   - Route process piping
   - Weld pipe joints
   - Install valves
   - Add supports/hangers

4. **Instrumentation & Electrical**
   - Run cable trays
   - Install instruments
   - Wire control panels
   - PLC programming

5. **Testing & Commissioning**
   - Pressure testing
   - Leak testing
   - Functional testing
   - Factory Acceptance Test (FAT)

## 6. E-HOUSE (ELECTRICAL HOUSE) FABRICATION

### 6.1 What is an E-House?
Pre-fabricated, walk-in enclosure housing electrical equipment (switchgear, MCC, transformers, control systems).

### 6.2 E-House Components
1. **Steel Structure** - Frame, walls, roof
2. **Cladding** - Insulated panels
3. **Electrical Equipment** - Switchgear, MCC, VFDs
4. **HVAC** - Air conditioning, ventilation
5. **Fire Protection** - Detection, suppression
6. **Accessories** - Lighting, emergency exit, marshalling racks

### 6.3 Fabrication Process
1. **Structural Fabrication**
   - Frame welding
   - Floor installation
   - Roof fabrication

2. **Wall & Roof Panels**
   - Insulated sandwich panels
   - Weatherproof sealing
   - Cutouts for equipment

3. **Equipment Installation**
   - Switchgear positioning
   - Bus duct routing
   - Transformer placement
   - Control system installation

4. **Systems Integration**
   - Power distribution
   - Control wiring
   - Communication networks
   - Safety systems

5. **Testing**
   - High voltage testing
   - Insulation resistance
   - Functional testing
   - FAT with client

## 7. FABRICATION PRODUCTIVITY FACTORS

### 7.1 Material Factors
| Material | Weldability | Cutting Speed | Difficulty |
|----------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| Carbon Steel | Excellent | Fast | Low |
| SS 304 | Good | Medium | Medium |
| SS 316 | Good | Medium | Medium |
| Duplex SS | Fair | Slow | High |
| Inconel | Poor | Slow | Very High |

### 7.2 Thickness Factors
| Thickness (mm) | Cutting Time | Weld Prep | Weld Time |
|----------------|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| 3-6 | Fast | Minimal | Low |
| 6-12 | Normal | Bevel | Medium |
| 12-25 | Slow | Double bevel | High |
| 25-50 | Very slow | Complex | Very high |
| 50+ | Special equip | Special prep | Excessive |

### 7.3 Welding Process Productivity (kg/hour)
| Process | Deposition Rate | Efficiency |
|---------|-----------------|------------|
| SMAW | 1-3 | Low |
| GTAW | 0.5-1.5 | Very Low |
| GMAW | 2-5 | High |
| FCAW | 3-8 | High |
| SAW | 5-15 | Very High |

### 7.4 Man-Hour Estimation Factors
1. **Material handling** - 10-15% of total hours
2. **Fit-up** - 20-30% of total hours
3. **Welding** - 40-50% of total hours
4. **NDT** - 5-10% of total hours
5. **Surface treatment** - 10-15% of total hours
6. **Testing** - 5-10% of total hours

## 9. SAFETY IN FABRICATION

### 9.1 Hazards
1. **Welding** - Arc flash, fumes, burns
2. **Cutting** - Sparks, noise, heat
3. **Material Handling** - Crushed by, struck by
4. **Working at Height** - Falls
5. **Confined Spaces** - Oxygen deficiency

### 9.2 PPE Requirements
- Welding helmet/shield
- Fire-resistant clothing
- Safety boots (steel toe)
- Gloves (appropriate type)
- Hearing protection
- Respiratory protection
